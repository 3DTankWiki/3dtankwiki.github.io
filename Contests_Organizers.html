Contests' Administrators

Contest Administrators performed the same functions as Contest Organizers but were empowered with greater authority. If you wanted to know why you had not won in a specific contest, provide input, or make a complaint about a helper's work, you had to forward it to an Administrator.

In the list of the Forum Members their names were highlighted in a gradient lime green and green-yellow.

Contests' Organizers

Contest Organizers were the workforce, the creative mind, and caretakers of the Community Contests Section. By acting as the caretakers of the community contests section, Contest Organizers were tasked with addressing any concerns or queries in addition to gathering any feedback that could arise pertaining to contests.

They were responsible for the preparation and organization of new contests by utilizing their creative minds to come up with new and original ideas while also regularly hosting chat contests in the in-game "Events" chat lobby. All in an effort to plan ahead and keep the community busy with new challenges.

In the list of the Forum Members their names were highlighted in a gradient lime green and green-yellow.

Contests' Organizers Candidate

Contest Organizer Candidates were users who had shown positive characteristics within the community and decided to try out as Contest Organizers themselves. These candidates were on a trial period while being trained and guided, and possessed the equal authority of a Contest Organizer, but were under closer supervision.

Once their trial period was over, all of their work would be reviewed and they would either graduate to become full Contest Organizers or return to the position of a player.

Parkour Contests' Organizers

Parkour Contest Organizers worked alongside regular Contest Organizers as the workforce, the creative mind, and caretakers of the Community Contests Section. They were responsible for the preparation and organization of new parkour contests by utilizing their creative minds and expertise in the parkour scene to create a variety of easy and difficult tasks for all parkourists.

From going into the battles themselves and working out the trick in addition to actually completing the trick, these organizers had to have an extreme amount of patience, time, and creativity.

In the list of the Forum Members their names were highlighted in white.
