Tanks in real life

see also: Contests and Fan Art



«

The task: create tanks from Tanki Online in real life out of common items.

»
— Forum topic for the contest


Tanks in real life was a contest in which participants had to create a tank that resembles a turret + hull combination from Tanki Online; the tank must be made out of common items you find around you, be that pencils, coins, matchsticks or anything else! Make sure to use physical objects only, drawings are not allowed. The winners were judged based on creativity, how closely the creation resembles a tank from Tanki Online, and originality.

Tanks in real life was very similar to the Tanki Craft contest; the only difference was a little less freedom on the kind of materials used in making the tank and how the entries had to be submitted.

The basic rules for Tanks in real life were as follows:

  • The tank must be made out of common items. Tanks made out of paper cutouts, wood carvings/cutouts, 3d prints, Lego etc will not be considered.
  • The tanks may not be painted in any way.
  • The photo must include your nickname. The best way to do this is to write your nickname on a piece of paper and place it next to the tank you created. You must also include the real names of the Turret/Hull you created in the same manner.
  • You must provide at least 2 photos of your tank from different angles.


Prizes

  • 1st Place: 100 000 crystals;
  • 2nd Place: 75 000 crystals;
  • 3rd Place: 50 000 crystals;
  • Consolation prize for worthy entries which didn't make it to any of the other places: 10 000 crystals.
