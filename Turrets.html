Turrets are a part of a tank in Tanki Online, alongside Hulls, Drones, Paints, and Modules.

By default, turrets can be rotated with Z and X, centered with C, and fired with SPACE. Some turrets also have a second firing mode that requires holding down the SPACE key.


Note: Even when hiding your hull behind cover, you may still take damage if projectiles hit the tank turret.

General information

Turrets can be received from Containers and in the special Early access section in the Shop. Some special turrets are exceptions; they appear in the game only for the duration of events or in some battle modes. They are automatically equipped in certain battle modes, so it is impossible* to get one in the garage.

* - some special accounts may have the special turrets.

In battles, it is possible to protect yourself from damage of a certain turret. For that, modules can be equipped in the garage. There is a separate module for each turret (except for special turrets).

Your turret's appearance and shot effect can be changed a little bit by equipping one of the available skins or shot effects. Also, augments that influence certain turret characteristics can be used.

Basic turrets mechanics

Every turret has its own unique gameplay. Some parameters are present in all turrets, and some are unique for a certain one. Main mechanics:

  • Critical Damage: turrets have a chance to deal critical damage, which is higher than normal.
  • Distance damage: some types of turrets deal damage depending on the distance to the target. Full damage is dealt up to a certain range (for example, if it is stated that range of max damage is 50 m, range of min. damage is 100 m, and weak damage percent is 20%, that means that on distance from 50 m to 100 m, damage will linearly decrease from 100% to 20%).
  • Range: melee and some medium-range weapons have an absolute range. The projectiles only reach up to this distance, beyond which no more damage is done.
  • Projectile speed: most of the turrets in the game have projectiles which have limited speed. This speed may be constant, increase, or decrease while flying. In the last cases, the turret also receives two extra parameters: initial projectile speed and time to reach final projectile speed.
    • Time to reach final projectile speed determines the acceleration or deceleration of the projectile while flying.
  • Ricochet: Some weapons' projectiles can ricochet off solid objects if the angle of impact is small enough. An exception is the Ricochet weapon, whose projectiles can ricochet at any angle. After bouncing, the projectiles' speed is set to the launch speed.
  • Splash damage: some turret projectiles explode when they hit a tank or a map surface. This deals damage to enemies in a small radius around the explosion.

Turrets upgrade

Every turret has 7 modification levels. Upgrading starts from the first level (Mk1), the weakest modification. All turrets can be upgraded to Mk7 for Crystals or a small amount of Rubies. But not everything can be bought immediately: to unlock a certain modification level, it is required to reach the corresponding rank when the next modification is unlocked.

Every modification can be upgraded until the next one is available. Max modification has the “MAX” title.

How turrets looked with the old graphics (until 9/6/2012) and what characteristics they had until the rebalance (12/17/2012) can be seen in the Turrets before the rebalance article.


Melee Range Turrets

see also: Melee-Range Turrets: How to Choose, How to Use


Melee range turrets have a very limited range. To use them effectively, it is required to be able to be in the right place at the right time and make direct contact.

Firebird

Close range turret. Sets enemies on fire, dealing additional damage.

On the narrow city streets, when the battle looks more like an ant farm, there’s no better weapon than the «Firebird» turret. It is the ideal turret for limited areas with no space to maneuver. Equally wonderful at putting slow heavy tanks on fire and crisping up fast light hulls.

Theoretically, a freon stream of the «Freeze» turret can extinguish the fire. In practice, you just yawn for a second and your tank has already burnt like a match.

Freeze

Close range turret. Freezes enemies, slowing down hulls and turrets.

Covering the opponent with a chemical compound based on freon, the «Freeze» turret damages and freezes the target, slowing all of its movement. The ability to fully freeze the enemy and escape its firing range allows fighting more powerful tanks.

The most important thing to remember - don't waste your fuel supply on snowball games with your allies. It's totally fun, of course, but battle tanks have more important things to do.

Isida

Close range emitter. Can repair allied tanks.

The «Isida» turret uses a magnetic beam to transport nanobots over a distance of several dozen meters. Nanobots can easily recognize the target and either quickly dismantle the armor into molecules or gradually repair it. This way, the weapon deals damage to enemies and repairs allies, which is especially important in team battles.

The research in quantum physics and nanotechnology has gifted an impressive set of abilities to a simple welding machine straight from the factory. If only it could be used to repair your own tank.

Tesla

Close range emitter. Attacks multiple enemies with chain lightning.

The «Tesla» turret can use any tank as a conductor for its chain lightning, including allied tanks that you can hide behind, and still reach the enemy with electricity. The fewer the targets hit by chain lightning simultaneously, the greater the damage to each individual target is.

An unforeseen short circuit on the test bench showed the rarest phenomenon - a ball of lightning. Ignoring all the safety regulations, engineers shot a chain of lightning straight in the direction of the ball lightning. Lightning struck the ball and continued, reaching the nearest hangar, hitting a whole battalion of brand new tanks inside.

Hammer

Short range turret. Uses shrapnel, which ricochets and damages several enemies at once.

The «Hammer» turret uses a shrapnel projectile with tungsten arrows that ricochet off surfaces and can hit several enemies at once. The arrows have low accuracy when firing at long distances, so you need to get closer to the opponent. The turret also has a loading drum magazine for three fast shots. Reloading is available only after the magazine is completely fired.

Due to its low mass, each tungsten arrow can ricochet only once. Usually, it's enough to get the enemy hiding behind a corner or to kick off its aim by firing at the enemy from the side. Though, you can also ricochet the shot back into yourself, depending on how lucky you are.

Medium Range Turrets

see also: Medium-Range Turrets: How to Choose, How to Use


Medium range turrets may not have a range limit, but their efficiency decreases over distance. To use them effectively, it is required to keep an enemy on the edge of the effective distance, letting them neither get into range for close quarter combat nor out of range of maximum damage.


Twins

Double-barreled short range turret. Has a very rapid firing speed, but slow-flying rounds.

The rapid-fire double-barreled plasmagun is ideal for dynamic fights at medium and short ranges. A plasma explosion will turn the enemy into a pile of molten iron in no time. It is necessary to observe safety measures and avoid close fights or you can easily melt down your own tank.

The cooling system of plasma chargers and capacitor recharge are designed to be so fail-proof, that some tankers manage to put different questionable objects (e.g. a brick) on the firing trigger to fire it non-stop. Thanks to that, evil rumors describe the sound of this turret shooting as an additional damaging factor, which annoys not only the enemies but allies as well.

Ricochet

Short range turret. Plasma projectiles will ricochet and damage enemies even out of your field of vision.

The «Ricochet» plasmagun fires plasma projectiles coated with a "smart" minus-field. the projectile will explode when it hits a tank, but if it hits a solid obstacle, it will bounce off and continue its flight. This way, you can hit targets even if you can't see them.

To mass-produce this turret, a team of 22 specialists worked for 3 years straight in an underground bunker without permission to see the sky. Unfortunately, it was impossible to solve one safety issue completely: plasma rounds can easily bounce back after ricocheting. The "smart" minus-field is clever, but not clever enough to distinguish enemy tanks from the user.

Vulcan

Medium range rapid-fire turret. A stack of six barrels allow an incredibly fast rate of fire.

This automatic multibarrel rapid-fire turret is capable of peppering enemies with armor-piercing bursts. It takes some time to accelerate the rotation of the barrel block. It is clumsy while shooting. While firing continuously, the tank can overheat and light itself on fire.

Turrets are cool - but sometimes, you want to shred the enemy into tiny pieces without thinking about such tactical nuances as reloading. This turret right here is the solution to all of your problems: A stack of six barrels, unbelievable firing speed, and an automated stabilizing system with armor-piercing rounds. Unlimited armor-piercing rounds.

«Smoky» is a legendary turret, made widely popular by how affordable and simple to maintain it is. Install it on a fast hull, and while sliding between shocked enemies, destroy each unwieldy heavy tank one by one.

Striker

Medium range missile launchers. Holding down the firing button will lock onto a target and launch a barrage of guided missiles.

Anti-tank missile launcher «Striker» requires significant time to lock onto a target. On the other hand, there is no chance of the target dodging the launched guided missiles. For dynamic battles, an option to directly shoot the rockets is also included. It's a bit wasteful, but in close combat, it can give you a decisive advantage in firing rate.

Since the dawn of warfare, the biggest threat to a tank was the anti-tank missile. That beastie could carefully deliver an explosive package to its target from a very long distance. Anti-tank turret «Striker» continues a century-old tradition of express explosion delivery to the tank and its armor.

Thunder

Medium-range turret. Highly explosive projectiles can hit several tanks at the same time.

The payload of the «Thunder» turret consists entirely of new-generation armor-piercing shells. Such rounds can hit a group of enemies, and the relatively fast reload of this turret will grant you an advantage over heavy enemy tanks. But be careful - in close quarters, it is very easy to damage your own tank.

This medium-range turret was designed with a rich history of using the legendary Smoky turret in mind. When combined with a fast hull, Thunder can turn into a deadly weapon in the hands of a seasoned tanker. Not-so-seasoned tankers do not exist. As a rule, they tend to blow themselves up very quickly and immediately cease to be considered tankers.

Long Range Turrets

see also: Long-Range Turrets: How to Choose, How to Use


Long range turrets don't have a range limit and their damage doesn't decrease as the distance increases. To use them effectively, it is enough to avoid close range combat.

Scorpion

Long-range turret. Holding down the firing button aims a swarm of artillery rockets.

The «Scorpion» turret has two firing modes, each with independent reloading system. In standard mode, the target is attacked by hypervelocity projectiles from the main long-range barrel. Holding down the firing button aims rocket salvo even if the target tries to hide behind an obstacle. Rockets attack the target from above, forcing it to leave shelter.

Now, the Scorpion turret is called an anti-tank artillery system. It wasn't always like that, the prototype didn't even have a real barrel - instead, engineers screwed on a rusty tube for camouflage. After the tests, the firecracker was replaced with a more frightening one! The caliber size kept increasing until the enemy stopped pretending to be smart and started pretending to be dead.

Magnum

Long range howitzer. Fires powerful explosive shells on a ballistic trajectory.

The «Magnum» turret fires shells in a pronounced ballistic trajectory. In standard settings, the barrel angle is fixed, but some augments allow you to exchange the turret rotation ability for the ability to control the vertical angle. When holding down the firing button, the guidance system draws the expected trajectory and the shell's impact point. Each shell has a large blast radius.

A true magnum opus of artistic tank construction. The biggest caliber, which can fit onto a tank hull. The most destructive high-explosive shell, from what we could find. The longest barrel with the biggest barrel brake. With enough skill, though, you can hit enemies behind any cover and at any distance.

Railgun

Long-range turret. Can pierce through tanks by hitting multiple targets in one line.

A big caliber turret with a lightning fast barrel speed. Incredibly powerful and overwhelmingly precise kinetic projectile can make a hole in any tank, hitting several targets standing in the same line. Don't forget that «Railgun» also takes a long time to reload and needs a short preparation before firing every shot.

If medium-caliber turrets can no longer satisfy your thirst for creativity, then this toy is just perfect for you. It requires good skills not only in marksmanship, but also in tank positioning. Read-to-fire barrel will burn brighter than a holiday tree, which can be detected and used as an advantage by experienced enemies.

Gauss

Long range sniper turret. Holding down the firing button for some time activates the electromagnetic accelerator.

The most mobile sniper on the battlefield. It is equipped with exploding shots that can hit multiple tanks simultaneously. Keeping the enemy in sight for a while, you can activate the electromagnetic accelerator which is built in the barrel. A projectile fired in this way deals increased damage, hitting a group of targets at any distance.

The «Gauss» turret was born in the garages of tankers who were looking for ways to increase the effectiveness of Thunder against snipers. A distinctive feature of this turret is the antennas, which extend while the electromagnetic accelerator is preparing for the shot. This turret has been nicknamed «big-eared sniper» due to its peculiar appearance.

Shaft

Long-range sniper turret. Can enter sniper mode by holding the firing button.

A laser turret with a unique firing mode. By halting the tank in place, you can direct all the energy of the reactor to pump up the power of the laser shot. Damage in this mode will depend on how long you charge up the shot, and your tank will also become completely immobile. Focusing your shot on the enemy will also use an additional laser pointer, which can be detected by most modern tanks' optical systems.

A great choice for fans of sniper battles. The turret works as a modern multifunctional weapon system, with an astounding number of uses. It can brew coffee, order pizza, pay bills - but its main feature is the sniper scope. Seasoned tankers recommend never being in the sniper mode for long, or you risk opening your backside for a surprise enemy attack.

Special Turrets

Those are unique items that can't be purchased or permanently received in any way. Exception - items may be used by the game developers for testing and organizing events.

Terminator

Hasta la vista, baby!

Terminator is a special turret that sports the ability to fire in two different modes. Using the long dual barrels, it can send out concentrated bursts of energy that penetrate the armor of enemy tanks and deal damage from the inside out. If cool effects aren't your thing, Terminator can also deploy its rocket launch system from the sides, lock on to enemies with a laser, and shoot a salvo consisting of six rockets.

Snowman

Snowman

The snowman turret is a special turret that launches deadly snowballs to unfortunate enemies nearby. It was accidentally created during a horrible blizzard before Christmas. One of Magnum's old prototypes was thrown away and left outside in a junkyard. At the beginning of the blizzard, there was a coolant leak that caused the snow on the prototype to be unmeltable. The owners of the junkyard found it was still functioning and thought it to be a perfect turret for the snowy season. When trying to clean the barrel from the snow they figured out that the snow-covered mines that were already loaded in the prototype could still be used as ammunition. In the end, it was decided to install it on a hull and decorate it to a snowman with a carrot, lighted branches, and a bucket.

Tomato Gun

Tomato Gun

The Tomato Gun is a modified version of Magnum, and is effectively a re-skinned Snowman turret. Its barrel elevation is slightly higher than Magnum's, and the speed of its shells is decreased. This results in Tomato Gun being a mid-range turret. On critical hits, it applies multiple status effects.
